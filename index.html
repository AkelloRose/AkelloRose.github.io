Key Findings

Insight 01 · Overall Performance
Profit Margin Below Industry Benchmark
At 12.47%, the store retains only 12 cents per dollar earned. Retail benchmarks suggest 20–30% is healthy. This flags potential over-discounting or high-cost product lines dragging profitability.
Insight 02 · Regional Gap
Central Region Is the Problem Area
West leads at 14.94% margin while Central sits at only 7.92% — a 4.55% gap below the store average despite generating $501K in sales. Heavy discounting is the likely culprit.
Insight 03 · Product
Furniture Drags the Entire Business
Furniture's margin is just 2.49% versus Technology and Office Supplies at 17%. Tables sub-category alone loses $17,725 on $206K in sales — selling at a loss on every order.
Insight 04 · Customers
Vast Customer Profitability Gap
Tamara Chand generates $8,981 profit at 47.14% margin. Cindy Stewart costs the business $6,626 at -116% margin. The same product is being sold at very different discount levels.

Recommendations

1
Conduct a discount audit in the Central region
Investigate why Central's margin is nearly half of West's despite similar sales volume. Identify products where discounts exceed 20% and calculate the profit loss.
2
Reprice or discontinue the Tables sub-category
The business loses $17,725 on Tables despite $206K in sales. Either raise prices, renegotiate supplier costs, or exit this sub-category.
3
Prioritise retention of high-margin customers
Tamara Chand and Raymond Buch generate 46%+ margins. Loyalty initiatives targeting this tier yield the highest return on retention investment.
4
Explore growth opportunities in the South
South has the lowest sales but a reasonable 11.93% margin. It is underserved, not inefficient — targeted marketing could unlock significant revenue growth.
